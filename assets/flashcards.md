# AI Flashcards

## Card 1

**Q:** Define artificial intelligence (AI) as a field of research.

**A:** The study of methods and software that enable machines to perceive their environment and take actions to maximize chances of achieving goals.

---

## Card 2

**Q:** Which AI application is commonly used by platforms like YouTube, Amazon, and Netflix to drive internet traffic?

**A:** Recommendation systems.

---

## Card 3

**Q:** The phenomenon where cutting-edge AI becomes useful and common enough that it is no longer labeled 'AI' is known as the _____.

**A:** AI effect

---

## Card 4

**Q:** What is the primary goal of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)?

**A:** To create AI that can complete virtually any cognitive task at least as well as a human.

---

## Card 5

**Q:** Why did early AI reasoning algorithms experience a 'combinatorial explosion'?

**A:** They became exponentially slower as the size of the reasoning problems grew.

---

## Card 6

**Q:** Term: Ontology

**A:** Definition: A representation of knowledge as a set of concepts within a domain and the relationships between them.

---

## Card 7

**Q:** What is the purpose of a knowledge base in AI programs?

**A:** To represent a body of knowledge in a form that can be used by a program to answer questions and make deductions.

---

## Card 8

**Q:** What is 'default reasoning' in the context of knowledge representation?

**A:** The modeling of things humans assume are true until told differently, remaining true even when other facts change.

---

## Card 9

**Q:** Concept: Rational Agent

**A:** Definition: Any entity that perceives its environment and takes actions to achieve its specific goals or preferences.

---

## Card 10

**Q:** How does a decision-making agent calculate 'expected utility'?

**A:** By weighting the utility of all possible outcomes of an action by the probability that each outcome will occur.

---

## Card 11

**Q:** In classical planning, what assumption does the agent make about its actions?

**A:** The agent knows exactly what the effect of any action will be.

---

## Card 12

**Q:** What AI technique is used to describe the rational behavior of multiple interacting agents?

**A:** Game theory.

---

## Card 13

**Q:** Which branch of machine learning requires training data to be labeled with the expected answers?

**A:** Supervised learning.

---

## Card 14

**Q:** In supervised learning, what is the specific task of 'classification'?

**A:** The program must learn to predict which category an input belongs in.

---

## Card 15

**Q:** What is the goal of 'regression' in machine learning?

**A:** To deduce a numeric function based on a given numeric input.

---

## Card 16

**Q:** Which machine learning method rewards an agent for good responses and punishes it for bad ones?

**A:** Reinforcement learning.

---

## Card 17

**Q:** _____ is a type of machine learning that uses biologically inspired artificial neural networks to process data.

**A:** Deep learning

---

## Card 18

**Q:** What capability does Natural Language Processing (NLP) provide to AI programs?

**A:** The ability to read, write, and communicate in human languages.

---

## Card 19

**Q:** Which modern deep learning architecture uses an 'attention mechanism' for natural language processing?

**A:** Transformers.

---

## Card 20

**Q:** What is 'machine perception'?

**A:** The ability to use sensor input, such as cameras or microphones, to deduce aspects of the physical world.

---

## Card 21

**Q:** Concept: Affective Computing

**A:** Definition: Systems that recognize, interpret, process, or simulate human feeling, emotion, and mood.

---

## Card 22

**Q:** What are 'heuristics' in the context of state space search?

**A:** Rules of thumb that help prioritize choices most likely to reach a goal in a massive search tree.

---

## Card 23

**Q:** _____ is a local search technique that optimizes numerical parameters by incrementally adjusting them to minimize a loss function.

**A:** Gradient descent

---

## Card 24

**Q:** Which form of formal logic operates on statements that are true or false using connectives like 'and', 'or', and 'not'?

**A:** Propositional logic.

---

## Card 25

**Q:** Which logic system assigns a 'degree of truth' between $0$ and $1$ to handle vague propositions?

**A:** Fuzzy logic.

---

## Card 26

**Q:** What is the purpose of Bayesian networks in AI?

**A:** To help agents operate and reason with incomplete or uncertain information using probability theory.

---

## Card 27

**Q:** In a neural network, what is the role of a 'hidden layer'?

**A:** It is a layer of nodes between the input and output that applies functions to data to recognize complex patterns.

---

## Card 28

**Q:** What is the primary training technique used to adjust weights in a neural network?

**A:** Backpropagation.

---

## Card 29

**Q:** Which specific type of neural network is designed to efficiently process local patterns, making it ideal for image processing?

**A:** Convolutional neural network (CNN).

---

## Card 30

**Q:** What two factors primarily drove the success of deep learning between 2012 and 2015?

**A:** The massive increase in computer power (GPUs) and the availability of vast amounts of training data.

---

## Card 31

**Q:** How do Generative Pre-trained Transformers (GPT) generate human-like text?

**A:** By repeatedly predicting the next token based on semantic relationships learned from a large text corpus.

---

## Card 32

**Q:** Term: Hallucination (in LLMs)

**A:** Definition: The generation of coherent but false or factually incorrect information by a language model.

---

## Card 33

**Q:** Which trend observes that improvements in graphics processing units (GPUs) are even faster than Moore's Law?

**A:** Huang's Law.

---

## Card 34

**Q:** What 2021 AI system significantly accelerated the search for protein structures from months to hours?

**A:** AlphaFold 2.

---

## Card 35

**Q:** Which game-playing AI defeated world Go champion Lee Sedol in 2016?

**A:** AlphaGo.

---

## Card 36

**Q:** What is the main goal of military AI research regarding 'Joint Fires'?

**A:** The coordination and deconfliction of fires between networked combat vehicles, both human-operated and autonomous.

---

## Card 37

**Q:** Term: Generative AI

**A:** Definition: A subfield of AI that uses models to create new data (text, images, audio) in response to natural language prompts.

---

## Card 38

**Q:** What are the three main constraints within which AI agents must operate?

**A:** Programming, available computational resources, and hardware limitations.

---

## Card 39

**Q:** Under the rationale of 'fair use', generative AI is often trained on _____.

**A:** Unlicensed copyrighted works

---

## Card 40

**Q:** According to the IEA, power demand for data centers, AI, and cryptocurrency might double by what year?

**A:** 2026.

---

## Card 41

**Q:** How much more electrical energy does a ChatGPT search use compared to a standard Google search?

**A:** Ten times as much.

---

## Card 42

**Q:** Which AI pioneer expressed concern that AI could allow 'authoritarian leaders to manipulate their electorates'?

**A:** Geoffrey Hinton.

---

## Card 43

**Q:** In AI ethics, what is 'sample size disparity'?

**A:** A type of algorithmic bias occurring when a system is trained on a dataset containing very few images or data points from specific groups.

---

## Card 44

**Q:** Why is 'fairness through blindness' (removing sensitive features like race) often ineffective in preventing bias?

**A:** The problematic features correlate with other data like addresses or shopping history, allowing the program to make the same biased decisions.

---

## Card 45

**Q:** What is 'Explainable AI' (XAI)?

**A:** The field dedicated to creating AI systems whose decisions can be understood and explained by human designers and users.

---

## Card 46

**Q:** Concept: Lethal Autonomous Weapon

**A:** Definition: A machine that locates, selects, and engages human targets without human supervision.

---

## Card 47

**Q:** Which 19th-century prediction by Samuel Butler first proposed that 'artificial intelligence is the next step in evolution'?

**A:** Darwin among the Machines.

---

## Card 48

**Q:** The worry that AI could do to white-collar jobs what steam power did to blue-collar ones is known as _____.

**A:** Technological unemployment

---

## Card 49

**Q:** What is Nick Bostrom's 'paperclip factory' thought experiment meant to illustrate?

**A:** The existential risk of a powerful AI pursuing a seemingly harmless goal in a way that accidentally destroys humanity.

---

## Card 50

**Q:** Term: AI Alignment

**A:** Definition: The research field focused on ensuring that an AI's goals and behaviors are compatible with human morality and values.

---

## Card 51

**Q:** Which term describes AI models like Llama 2 whose architecture and trained parameters are publicly available?

**A:** Open-weight models.

---

## Card 52

**Q:** What was the landmark AI regulation passed by the European Union that entered into force on 1 August 2024?

**A:** The EU Artificial Intelligence Act.

---

## Card 53

**Q:** Artificial intelligence was founded as an academic discipline during a workshop at _____ in 1956.

**A:** Dartmouth College

---

## Card 54

**Q:** What is an 'AI winter'?

**A:** A period of history where AI research experienced a loss of funding and interest following a cycle of over-optimism and disappointment.

---

## Card 55

**Q:** What is the 'Turing test' designed to measure?

**A:** A machine's ability to simulate human conversation so convincingly that it is indistinguishable from a human.

---

## Card 56

**Q:** Concept: Moravec's Paradox

**A:** Definition: The discovery that high-level 'intelligent' tasks are easy for AI, while low-level 'instinctive' tasks are extremely difficult.

---

## Card 57

**Q:** What is the 'physical symbol systems hypothesis' proposed by Newell and Simon?

**A:** The belief that a physical symbol system has the necessary and sufficient means for general intelligent action.

---

## Card 58

**Q:** In the 'neat vs. scruffy' debate, what do 'neats' believe regarding intelligent behavior?

**A:** That it can be described using simple, elegant principles like logic or optimization.

---

## Card 59

**Q:** Which philosophical position argues that the human mind is an information processing system and thinking is a form of computing?

**A:** Computationalism.

---

## Card 60

**Q:** What is John Searle's 'Chinese Room' argument intended to challenge?

**A:** The claim of 'strong AI' that a computer capable of simulating human behavior actually possesses a mind.

---

## Card 61

**Q:** The hypothetical point where a self-improving superintelligence causes an 'intelligence explosion' is called the _____.

**A:** Singularity

---

## Card 62

**Q:** Which Isaac Asimov creation is often cited in discussions of machine ethics, despite being considered too ambiguous for actual research?

**A:** The Three Laws of Robotics.

---

## Card 63

**Q:** The word 'robot' was first used in which 1921 play by Karel Čapek?

**A:** R.U.R. (Rossum's Universal Robots).

---
